Country residents arriving in Australian by Lastest Data (July 2024)

In the first half of 2024, Australia saw a significant number of international arrivals, with 6,414,940 visitors recorded, represent about 25% of Australia’s population (26,638,544). This reflects strong tourism and international movement, with Australia continuing to be a key destination for travelers, businesspeople, and migrants.

The majority of visitors came from New Zealand and Asia region, as well as major global partners such as the United States, China. In addition, the large amount of citizen (over population) of countries such as Canada, Mongolia, France and within East/Southest Asia (Vietnam, Cambodia, Thailand, Malaysia, Japan) also reflects the impressive preference to be arriving to Australia. Overall, this underscore Australia’s prominence as a trending travel and migration hub in the Asia-Pacific region.

The past 10 years of Autralian arrivals and visa issued (2014 - 2024)

Over the last decade from 2014, Australia's visa issuance experienced significant fluctuations, reflecting broader global events and policy shifts. In 2019, the country reached a peak, issuing around 2 million visas in December within 5 main categories. The majority were from New South Wales and the most popular category is temporary visas, notably for visitors and immigrants from New Zealand. Also, Australia was and is always a preferable destination for international students pursuing higher education (over 60% of total student visa), reflecting across all states and regions.

However, the rising of the COVID-19 pandemic in early 2020 marked a turning point. Strict border closures led to a dramatic decline in visa issuances, with nearly no arrivals and visas granted during this period. This downturn persisted until late 2021, when gradual easing of restrictions allowed for a resurgence in visa applications and approvals. By the start of 2023, Australia's visa issuance had recovered robustly with similar visa trends as the previous period, signaling a revival in tourism and international education sectors.
